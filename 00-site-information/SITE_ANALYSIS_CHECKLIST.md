# Site Analysis Checklist

Use this checklist before starting detailed layout work.

## 1. Site Context

- Identify the site location using the Google Maps reference.
- Note nearby roads and possible access points.
- Identify surrounding land uses.
- Note any visible neighbouring developments.
- Identify possible noise, traffic, or security concerns.

## 2. Site Size and Scale

- Confirm the site is being treated as a 24-hectare site.
- Convert the site size into square metres for scale thinking.
- Estimate how much land could be needed for parking, circulation, buildings, service yards, and green/buffer areas.

## 3. Access and Movement

- Identify possible public vehicle entrances.
- Identify possible staff entrances.
- Identify possible delivery and service entrances.
- Identify where heavy vehicles may need to move.
- Consider emergency vehicle access.
- Separate pedestrian flow from service and workshop vehicle flow where possible.

## 4. Public, Private, and Restricted Zones

- Identify public-facing zones.
- Identify staff-only zones.
- Identify operational zones.
- Identify high-security zones.
- Identify areas requiring controlled access.
- Consider security checkpoints or gatehouses.

## 5. Noise and Activity Separation

- Keep doctors' rooms away from mechanical workshop noise.
- Keep restaurants away from industrial or service-yard activity.
- Keep high-security industrial areas separated from general public areas.
- Consider buffer zones between incompatible uses.

## 6. Parking and Circulation

- Consider customer parking.
- Consider staff parking.
- Consider workshop customer drop-off areas.
- Consider loading and delivery areas.
- Consider auction event parking.
- Consider separate parking for restricted industrial staff.

## 7. Initial Deliverables

Each student or team must produce:

- Site location summary
- Site analysis notes
- Public/private/restricted zoning notes
- Vehicle and pedestrian movement notes
- List of assumptions
- List of questions for Ben
- First zoning sketch or diagram
